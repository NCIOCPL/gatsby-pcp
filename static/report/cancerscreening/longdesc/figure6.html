Bar graphs representing percentages of average lifetime risk of being diagnosed with different cancer types depending on condition. For Lynch Syndrome:

  • Colorectal cancer: 4% risk for the general population and 56% for those with Lynch syndrome
  • Endometrial cancer: 3.1% risk for the general population and 54% for those with Lynch syndrome
  • Ovarian cancer: 1.2% risk for the general population and 22% for those with Lynch syndrome
  • Gastric cancer: 1% risk for the general population and 13% for those with Lynch syndrome.

For carriers of a BRCA gene mutation:

  • Breast cancer: 13% risk for the general population and 72% for those with a BRCA mutation
  • Prostate cancer: 12.1% risk for the general population and 60% for those with a BRCA mutation
  • Ovarian cancer: 1.2% risk for the general population and 44% for those with a BRCA mutation
  • Pancreatic cancer: 1.6% risk for the general population and 10% for those with a BRCA mutation.
